The Security Gap

Perimeter walls and community guards leave your home blind.

Many homeowners rely entirely on estate guards and a front gate while the actual home remains vulnerable at front doors, ground-floor windows, rear access points, balconies, and utility areas. Traditional home alarms can also create 2 AM anxiety when wind, pets, or poor placement trigger false alarms. Without visual verification and a clear response process, you are left guessing whether an alert is real.

The Urban Eye Solution

The Three Lines of Protection.

Urban Eye does not place random sensors. We build a residential security system designed to deter, detect, and delay intruders before they reach your family, while giving you clear mobile alerts and practical response steps.

First Line: Outdoor Protection

We secure external approach points such as gates, driveways, garages, utility rooms, gardens, and yard movement using outdoor detection and strategic CCTV planning.

Second Line: Building Perimeter

We protect the physical shell of your home: main doors, ground-floor windows, roof access, vents, balconies, and other accessible openings.

Third Line: Interior & Assets

We protect interior rooms, safes, and high-value items using pet-immune sensors and visual verification options so you can confirm what triggered the alarm.

FAQs

Common residential security questions.

Good home security should reduce panic, avoid false alarms, and give your family clear control.

Will my pets constantly trigger the alarm?

No. We can use pet-immune sensors designed to ignore the movement of domestic animals while still reacting to human intruders. This allows you to secure your home while pets move freely in selected areas.

Does the Three Lines approach work for apartments too?

Yes, but it is scaled to your needs. For middle-floor apartments, we usually focus on doors, accessible windows, and interior motion. For ground-floor or top-floor apartments, we may also review balconies, roof access, or shared corridors.

Do I really need a site survey first?

Yes. Every home is different. A site survey helps identify real blind spots and recommend the right equipment, preventing overspending on unnecessary devices or leaving important access points unprotected.
