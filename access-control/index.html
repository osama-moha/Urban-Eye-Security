The Security Gap

Shared keys and unmanaged entry points create blind spots.

In offices, apartment blocks, shops, clinics, warehouses, and gated properties, physical keys and uncontrolled PIN sharing make it difficult to know who entered, when they entered, and whether they should have entered at all.

No accountability Visitor books and copied keys do not give you a reliable record of movement.
Weak restriction Sensitive rooms and operational areas are harder to protect with traditional locks alone.
Slow response When access is abused, it is harder to investigate and act quickly.

FAQs

Common access control questions.

Can I give temporary access to contractors or cleaning staff?

Yes. Temporary credentials or codes can be configured for specific users, areas, or time windows and later revoked.

Can access control be used on gates or barriers?

Yes. Depending on the site and hardware, selected gates, barriers, turnstiles, and controlled doors can be integrated into one plan.

Is access control only for big office buildings?

No. It can also work well for residential estates, apartment blocks, clinics, warehouses, shops, and other properties that need controlled movement.
