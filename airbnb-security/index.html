The Short-Stay Security Gap

Guests need privacy. Hosts still need control.

Short-stay properties face a difficult balance. Metal keys can be copied, previous guests can retain access, unauthorized visitors may enter, and vacant units can remain exposed between bookings.

At the same time, hosts must respect guest privacy. Security should protect the property, not make guests feel watched inside the home.

Smart Host Framework

Complete control, from check-in to check-out.

Urban Eye designs the security around how your property actually operates: guest arrival, access codes, exterior entry points, vacant periods, emergency alerts, and support after deployment.

01

Keyless access and secure check-in

Replace copied keys with temporary guest codes that can be changed, revoked, or scheduled around check-in and check-out times.

Useful for apartments, guest houses, serviced units, and hosts managing multiple listings or remote check-ins.

02

Privacy-conscious exterior visibility

Position cameras around exterior entrances, gates, parking, corridors, and driveways where visibility is useful without monitoring private indoor spaces.

Helps deter unauthorized access, verify entry events, and support response while respecting guest privacy expectations.

03

Guest-friendly alerts

Use smoke, fire, panic, leak, and environmental alerts to protect guests and the property without relying on intrusive indoor surveillance.

The goal is simple: detect serious issues early and give the host a clear next step before damage escalates.

04

Vacant property protection

Between bookings, your property can be more vulnerable. Door alerts, intrusion detection, exterior cameras, and monitoring options help protect the unit when nobody is there.

Best for hosts with frequent guest turnover, high-value furnished units, or properties left vacant between bookings.
