The Warehouse Security Gap

Most warehouse losses happen where movement is poorly controlled.

Large perimeters, loading bays, high-bay racking, stockrooms, and vehicle movement create blind spots that basic cameras and lone guards cannot fully control. A passive CCTV system may only show what disappeared after the loss has already happened.

The bigger risk is often operational: weak staff entry control, poor loading dock oversight, unverified outbound movement, exposed high-value cages, and unclear response procedures after an alarm.

FAQs

Common warehouse security questions.

Warehouse security requires more planning than a standard office or retail installation because movement, stock value, and operational zones change throughout the day.

Can you secure loading bays and roller shutters?

Yes. We can review loading bay routines, roller shutter exposure, dispatch points, and vehicle access so the right mix of CCTV, alarm detection, access control, and procedures can be recommended.

Can you help reduce internal shrinkage?

Yes. Urban Eye can help improve visibility around staff entry points, restricted rooms, stockrooms, high-value cages, and exit procedures so movement is easier to verify and review.

Will wireless alarms work in a large warehouse?

It depends on the layout, walls, racking, distance, and interference. That is why we review signal requirements, range extension, power backup, and detector placement during the site survey.
